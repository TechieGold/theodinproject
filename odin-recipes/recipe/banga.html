Declicious Banga Soup

Ingredients

  • 800g palm fruits
  • Beef
  • Dry fish
  • smoked mackerel (titus fish)
  • 1-2 bulbs of red onion
  • tablespoons ground crayfish
  • seasoning cubes
  • 1 small lump of ogiri okpei
  • 1 small lump of ogiri okpei
  • Scent leaves

Before you prepare Ofe Akwu

  1. Soak the dry fish in water to soften.
    When soft, clean, debone and break into small pieces.
  2. Chop the onions
  3. Pound the Ogiri Okpei and pepper in a mortar till smooth.
  4. Rinse and boil the palm fruits till done then pound in a mortar till the flesh forms a smooth pulp.
  5. Separate the palm nuts from the pulp then rinse the pulp in as hot water as your hands can handle to get a thick concentrate.
    See the video below for how to do this. When done, set aside to settle.
  6. Pick the scent leaves and slice.
  7. Smoke the mackerel in your oven, Then debone and break into large pieces.

Directions for cooking the Ofe Akwu

  1. Boil the beef with the seasoning cubes and half of the chopped onions till done.
    For best results, I grill the meat in the oven.
  2. Slowly decant the palm fruit concentrate into the pot you will use to cook the Ofe Akwu
    making sure the unwanted sediments do not go into the pot.
  3. Add the beef stock, the grilled meat, dry fish, the remaining onions, ogiri okpei and pepper and crayfish
  4. Cover and start cooking. When it boils, continue cooking till you see some red oil floating on top.
  5. Add the smoked mackerel and scent leaves.
  6. Cover and once it boils again, itâ€™s done! Taste and add salt if necessary.
  7. Serve with boiled rice
